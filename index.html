Why Most Google Ads Campaigns Fail for Home Service Businesses

Before I explain what I do, here is what typically goes wrong — and why it keeps happening.

01

Budget Going to the Wrong Searches

Without a proper negative keyword list, your ads show up for job seekers, DIY searches, and people who will never book. You pay for every click — whether it leads to a customer or not.

02

Conversion Tracking That Does Not Work

If Google cannot see which clicks turned into real leads, it cannot find you more of them. Broken or missing tracking means your budget is being spent with no clear direction.

03

Wrong Bidding Strategy at the Wrong Time

Smart Bidding needs enough conversion data before it can perform. Set it up too early and the algorithm guesses — costing you more per lead while delivering fewer results.

04

No Clear Campaign Structure

One campaign for all services means zero visibility. You cannot see what is working, what is wasting money, or where to cut and where to scale.

These are not theoretical problems. They are the exact issues I identify in almost every account I audit. My entire process is built around eliminating them — systematically, in the right order.

How I Work — The Six-Phase Campaign Management System

This is the same framework I apply whether I am managing a single local client account or working across a portfolio of accounts within a digital marketing agency. Every phase is structured, documented, and built for consistent, repeatable results.

1

Discovery and Business Analysis

I start by understanding your business — calculating your Maximum Allowable CPA based on job value, profit margin, and close rate. This number drives every decision in the account. I also map your service area and identify your highest-value service lines.

2

Competitor and Keyword Research

I analyse who is running ads in your area, what they are doing, and where the gaps are. Then I build your keyword list around high-intent searches and create a negative keyword list from day one to block job seekers, DIY queries, and irrelevant traffic.

3

Campaign Build and Tracking Setup

Campaigns are structured by service line with targeted ad copy and all extensions configured. Full conversion tracking is set up via GTM, GA4, and call tracking — and verified before anything goes live. No campaign launches with unconfirmed tracking.

4

Launch and Data Collection

Campaigns launch on Max Clicks with a calculated CPC limit. This phase builds clean data without overspending. I monitor search terms, budget pacing, and early conversions daily during the first two weeks.

5

Optimisation and Smart Bidding Transition

Once enough data is collected, I move to Max Conversions. After 30 or more conversions, Target CPA is introduced and reduced gradually — 10 to 15 percent every two weeks — to lower cost per lead without disrupting performance.

6

Reporting and Scaling

Monthly reports cover CPA trends, conversion volume, Impression Share, lead quality, and a clear action plan for the next month. Budget is reallocated toward the best-performing service lines to maximise return as the account scales.
